I am Jiaxuan Li 李嘉轩 (pronounced as Lǐ Jiā Xuān ). I just graduate from Department of Astronomy, Peking University with a Bachelor's degree of Science. I'm currently having a gap year and working at Kavli Institute for Astronomy and Astrophysics, Peking University (KIAA, PKU) as an research assistant. I will be a graduate student at Department of Astrophysical Sciences, Princeton University in 2021.

I'm interested in a variety of topics in astronomy and astrophysics, such as galaxy formation and evolution, low surface brightness astrophysics, cosmology, deep sky surveys, supernova, data science and astronomical instrumentation. Currently I'm working with the data from cutting-edge deep sky surveys (such as Hyper Suprime-Camera (HSC) and Dragonfly) to study low surface brightness objects in our Universe. I also had experience on studying galaxy quenching mechanism utilizing MaNGA IFU data. In the future, I'm eager to develop expertise on various topics of galaxy and cosmology.

On my homepage, you can find more than my research. I'm a big fan of any kind of music, from Indian Sitar to Chopin and Rachmaninoff. I became an amateur astronomer since I was in sixth grade in primary school. I also took photos for a variety of objects, not only in the sky but also around myself.
